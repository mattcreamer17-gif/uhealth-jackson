The context

South Florida's only academic medical center operates as two balance sheets and one campus. UHealth carries the faculty and the research pull, with Bascom Palmer holding its U.S. News No. 1 ophthalmology rank for 24 consecutive years, Sylvester's NCI designation renewed "Outstanding" in July 2024, and the Frost Institute for Data Science and Computing running the infrastructure core for NIH's AIM-AHEAD consortium.

Jackson Health System carries the 1,550-bed safety-net load, the Ryder Trauma Center, and the Public Health Trust governance that defines how any new technology reaches the floor. Dr. Dipen Parekh took the UHealth CEO role in June 2025, and David Zambrana takes the Jackson CEO role on June 1, 2026, with outpatient growth as his first public priority.

About 60% of most workflow problems are traditional code and database work. About 30% are rule-based logic. About 10% are genuine AI problems where a large model earns its keep.
The frame

Eduba works on computational orchestration. Teams that skip the 60/30/10 distinction spend AI budgets on the wrong layer. Inside a dual-entity academic medical center, the cost of that mistake is higher, because every wasted cycle meets a governance committee before it ships.

Applied to UHealth/Jackson, the frame points to pre-authorization, intake, chart preparation, and documentation-to-billing as the workflows where orchestration design pays back before the first PHI review. It also points to the areas where a model should not sit: clinical decisions that belong to clinicians, documentation fields that belong in structured code, and any decision that carries legal exposure absent a human signature.

Closest precedent

KPMG UK, one of the Big Four, is the closest engagement precedent. 40+ executives trained on orchestration design and decision-layer mapping, in a regulated-industry setting with a senior audience and heavy internal approvals. That is the closest public analogue to how UHealth and Jackson evaluate net-new work.

Eduba partners with NLP Logix for work that sits below the orchestration layer. NLP Logix has been in machine learning since 2011 and runs over 150 data scientists, so when a surfaced workflow extends into production data-pipeline engineering, the downstream partner is already in place.
